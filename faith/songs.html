I don’t listen to a ton of music, but when I do I especially love music with lyrics that pay special attention to flow and word choice. There’s a lot that goes into making spoken/sung word sound good. So here's three songs that I like, with about 400 words on each.

The Silly One

This is the band that made Stacy’s Mom. It’s even the same album. Bet you weren’t expecting to see them up first. The album is broadly about American Suburban life squeezing joy out of you, with trademarked Millennial self-awareness. These three short verses each describe some sad event. I think of it as basically three separate poems.

He was killed by a cellular phone explosion
They scattered his ashes across the ocean
The water was used to make baby lotion
The wheels of promotion were set into motion

This opening verse is oddly existential. A man dies in a comical way (a phone exploding), and the ocean currents carry the ashes to a random factory that produces… baby lotion. Scattering ashes is usually meaningful. Baby lotion is so random and mundane it definitely robs meaning from these ashes. It’s like letting a butterfly go free and it immediately flies into a jet engine. At least a middle manager gets promoted because the factory runs well.

She lived alone in a small apartment
Across the street from the Health department
She left her pills in the glove compartment
That was the afternoon her heart went

Here a woman dies of a heart attack because she accidentally left her medication in her car. Her living alone is both sad and why no one saves her. But damn this verse got stuck in my head for like 4 months after I heard it the first time. It’s such an ear worm when put with the music.

I used to fly for United Airlines
Then I got fired for reading High Times
License expired in almost no time
Now I'm retired and I think that's fine

I always assumed “Reading High Times” meant smoking weed and not just reading the magazine (which is about weed). The poor guy got fired for it. Guy can’t catch a break. It’s implied this is the narrator of the song. Between all these stories there’s a chorus. The narrator (the pilot from the last verse) looks at these sad stories, looks at his spouse. Then he half smiles, and half gives up.

But the sun still shines in the summer time
I'll be yours if you'll be mine
I tried to change but I changed my mind
Think I'll have another glass of Mexican wine

This whole thing feels very tongue-in-cheek. The death at the beginning is pretty silly/weird. We describe this woman tragically dying but move on before it registers. A pilot gets fired for smoking weird and says “yeah i guess i’m retired now” It all just feels like it’s not taking itself seriously. Like I said earlier it’s very self aware and very millennial. It portrays an America where sad things happen and we can't really do anything about it. But we can half smile during it.
