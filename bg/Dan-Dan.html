What makes a Forgetful Fish Format?

(5 min. read)

A Eulogy for Skullbriar and his wife (link) is a format inspired by Forgetful Fish (AKA Dan-Dan). My brother and I designed it together. Recently while cleaning out my room I found old notes from the beginning of the project.

The gist of the format was always a shared graveyard 1v1 format, with Skullbriar the Walking Grave starting the graveyard. Players will be fighting to resurrect Skullbriar to kill each other. And as the game goes on Skullbriar will get bigger and bigger. But there was lots of experimentation around that core. Here’s stuff I considered.

Ordeal of Heliod/Erebos/Thassa as payoffs for having a big skullbriar.

For a while the only source of removal was twenty copies of Abu-Jafar.

While the deck itself is mono white now using Jolted Awakes to resurrect Skullbrair. I also experimented with Helping Hand, and making the deck mono black with Unearth.

I also experimented with adding different counters to Skullbriar (If you give him flying it’s now extra swingy whoever has him)

Looking back on the notes I struggled a lot with card advantage. I was very focused on emulating parts of Forgetful Fish, where card advantage is basically the entire game. I think I was stuck in a holding pattern of just thinking and trying to copy my inspiration. No real progress was made until I just shuffled but a broken mess of cards and played with a friend. (I never made progress playtesting solo). And then we followed the fun. I don’t think the format became fun until very far into development, but we saw a light and followed it. I took what worked and rolled downhill. I wasn’t trying to fit my white peg into a blue hole.

During playtesting we tried having Bereaved Survivor and Skullbriar start the game in the graveyard together. As a joke I insinuated they were married. This young human was married to this horrific Nils Hamm monstrosity. Not only was this very funny, that’s where the most unique part of this format came from. It’s a Dan-Dan format, with lore. Once we had that premise it was easy to reinforce that theme with card choices. So there’s example #501 that playtesting and just trying things is very valuable.

You can read the Cuba Cobra (link) page if you’re curious about specific texture of the format. But here’s two highlights:
Bovine Intervention is the opposite of clean removal. Giving your opponent a free 2/2 is really brutal.
Bagels let players loop them with Jolted Awake and net energy. That extra energy can be dumped into Hexgold Slith. A first striking Hexgold Slith presents very interesting blocking decisions.

I think most Dan-Dan imitators focus on the fish himself. “Let’s replace the fish with lighting bolts.” is a thought had by about a million people. But anyone who’s played enough knows what makes the format fun is Memory Lapse. The core of the format is “memory lapse but shared library”. Skullbriar started as “Skullbriar but shared graveyard.”

So maybe a more useful heuristic is these formats aren’t about replacing dan-dan with something else, but “X Card plus shared Y” formats? For example Forgetful Fish is a shared library + Memory Lapse format. I expect many successful Dan-Dan imitators fall into this mold. Maybe “Terravore but shared graveyard”. Actually, here’s a 2d8 table with zones plus card types.

Key Card:
1 - Creature (big)
2 - Creature (small)
3 - Land
4 - Instant
5 - Sorcery
6 - Artifact/Enchantment
7 - Planeswalker
8 - Planechase

There’s a finite number of possible types of Dan-Dan like formats. Ex. Shared Library + Instant is the type Forgetful Fish falls into. Again, probably not useful for actually designing them but a fun academic exercise.

I got 5 & 5. You can tell I chose a random number because the same one came up both times. If I just picked something I wouldn’t have done that. Sorcery plus shared nonlands. Ok. Whenever a player plays nonland it goes into a shared battlefield. And the key card is a sorcery. Maybe… Sweltering Suns? There’s lots in the deck so anyone can destroy all the little creatures if they want. If you get a creature with 4 toughness now you and your opponent have a very unkillable creature? This is a completely untested idea but lets theorycraft because it’s fun. A funny quirk is that if you play a creature, your opponent is the first to attack with it because it loses summoning sickness on their turn. So its impossible to be aggressive and defense is impossible because your opponent can leave you with no blockers… Maybe the deck is all 2/2’s and ⅓’s with defender? It’s about making sure there’s more 2/2s on the board during your turn and more ⅓’s on your opponents turn? And anyone can wipe the board at any time? Maybe both players start at 1 life. It’s the end of the world. There’s fire raining from the sky, and there’s only two types of creatures left. Looking at scryfall I find these two cards. Once the werewolf shows up now players must always cast a spell to prevent the werewolf from killing a rampart.

Card advantage matters in Skullbriar, but if both players start at 1 life it doesn’t super matter here. Just find a way to break through once. I’m worried it won’t be interactive, just players dumping their hand, but ofc if you just dump your hand your opponent will have those resources too. This format sounds like it sucks, but the only way it won’t suck is to test it.
